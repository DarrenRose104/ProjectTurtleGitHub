The green turtle is one of the largest sea turtles and is the only herbivore among the different species.
Green Turtles are named for the greenish colour of their cartilage and fat, not their shells, They are also called have black turtles by the local communities.

Green turtles are mainly found in tropical and subtropical waters. They migrate long distances between feeding grounds and the beaches they were hatched.
They graze on seagrasses and algae, which maintains seagrass beds.
The seagrass green turtles graze upon are quickly digested which then becomes nutrients to many species of plants and animals that live in the seagrass system.
