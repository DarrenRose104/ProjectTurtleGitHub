General

Hawksbill turtles get their name from their narrow pointed beak. They have a distinctive pattern on their shells, overlapping scales that form a serrated-look on the edges. Due to this, the coloured and patterned shells are highly-valuable and commonly sold as "tortoiseshell" in markets.
Hawksbill turtles are found throughout tropical oceans, predominantly coral reefs. Their main food source is sponges, which they extract by using their narrow pointed beaks to extract them from crevices on the reef, however they also eat sea anemones and jellyfish.

Hawksbill Turtles grow up to 45 inches in shell length and 150 pounds in weight. The carapace or upper shell is heart shaped whilst they are young, and as they mature it elongates.
Hawksbills migrate incredible distances in order to move from feeding sites to move from feeding sites to nesting grounds, normally on tropical beaches. Female hawksbills return to the beaches where they were born to nest, every two to five years. This takes place in shallow waters close to the shore.
